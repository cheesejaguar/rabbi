# Proverbs (English)

## Chapter 1

**1:1** The proverbs of Solomon son of David, king of Israel:

**1:2** For learning wisdom and discipline;For understanding words of discernment;

**1:3** For acquiring the discipline for success,Righteousness, justice, and equity;

**1:4** For endowing the simple with shrewdness,The young with knowledge and foresight.

**1:5** —The wise, hearing them, will gain more wisdom;The discerning will learn to be adroit;

**1:6** For understanding proverb and epigram,The words of the wise and their riddles.

**1:7** The fear of GOD is the beginningabeginning Or “best part.” of knowledge;Fools despise wisdom and discipline.

**1:8** My son, heed the discipline of your father,And do not forsake the instruction of your mother;

**1:9** For they are a graceful wreath upon your head,A necklace about your throat.

**1:10** My son, if sinners entice you, do not yield;

**1:11** If they say, “Come with us,Let us set an ambush to shed blood,Let us lie in wait for the innocent(Without cause!)

**1:12** Like Sheol, let us swallow them alive;Whole, like those who go down into the Pit.

**1:13** We shall obtain every precious treasure;We shall fill our homes with loot.

**1:14** Throw in your lot with us;We shall all have a common purse.”

**1:15** My son, do not set out with them;Keep your feet from their path.

**1:16** For their feet run to evil;They hurry to shed blood.

**1:17** In the eyes of every winged creatureThe outspread net means nothing.

**1:18** But they lie in ambush for their own blood;They lie in wait for their own lives.

**1:19** Such is the fate of all who pursue unjust gain;It takes the life of its possessor.

**1:20** WisdombWisdom In Proverbs, wisdom is personified as a woman. cries aloud in the streets,Raises her voice in the squares.

**1:21** At the head of the busy streets she calls;At the entrance of the gates, in the city, she speaks out:

**1:22** “How long will you simple ones love simplicity,You scoffers be eager to scoff,You dullards hate knowledge?

**1:23** You are indifferent to my rebuke;I will now speak my mind to you,And let you know my thoughts.

**1:24** Since you refused me when I called,And paid no heed when I extended my hand,

**1:25** You spurned all my advice,And would not hear my rebuke,

**1:26** I will laugh at your calamity,And mock when terror comes upon you,

**1:27** When terror comes like a disaster,And calamity arrives like a whirlwind,When trouble and distress come upon you.

**1:28** Then they shall call me but I will not answer;They shall seek me but not find me.

**1:29** Because they hated knowledge,And did not choose fear of GOD;

**1:30** They refused my advice,And disdained all my rebukes,

**1:31** They shall eat the fruit of their ways,And have their fill of their own counsels.

**1:32** The tranquility of the simple will kill them,And the complacency of dullards will destroy them.

**1:33** But the one who listens to me will dwell in safety,Untroubled by the terror of misfortune.”


## Chapter 2

**2:1** My son, if you accept my wordsAnd treasure up my commandments;

**2:2** If you make your ear attentive to wisdomAnd your mind open to discernment;

**2:3** If you call to understandingAnd cry aloud to discernment,

**2:4** If you seek it as you do silverAnd search for it as for treasures,

**2:5** Then you will understand the fear of GOD And attain knowledge of God.

**2:6** For GOD grants wisdom;Knowledge and discernment are by God’s decree.

**2:7** Ability is reserved for the uprightAnd [God] is a shield for those who live blamelessly,

**2:8** Guarding the paths of justice,Protecting the way of those who are loyal.

**2:9** You will then understand what is right, just,And equitable—every good course.

**2:10** For wisdom will enter your mindAnd knowledge will delight you.

**2:11** Foresight will protect you,And discernment will guard you.

**2:12** It will save you from an evildoer’s ways,From a man who speaks duplicity—

**2:13** Those who leave the paths of rectitudeTo follow the ways of darkness,

**2:14** Who rejoice in doing evilAnd exult in an evildoer’s duplicity,

**2:15** All whose paths are crookedAnd who are devious in their course.

**2:16** It will save you from the forbiddenaforbidden Lit. “strange.” woman,From the alien woman whose talk is smooth,

**2:17** Who forsakes the companion of her youthAnd disregards the covenant of her God.

**2:18** Her house sinks down to Death,And her course leads to the shades.

**2:19** All who go to her cannot returnAnd find again the paths of life.

**2:20** So follow the way of the goodAnd keep to the paths of the just.

**2:21** For the upright will inhabit the earth,The blameless will remain in it.

**2:22** While the wicked will vanish from the landAnd the treacherous will be rooted out of it.


## Chapter 3

**3:1** My son, do not forget my teaching,But let your mind retain my commandments;

**3:2** For they will bestow on you length of days,Years of life and well-being.

**3:3** Let fidelity and steadfastness not leave you;Bind them about your throat,Write them on the tablet of your mind,

**3:4** And you will find favor and approbationIn the eyes of God and human beings.

**3:5** Trust in GOD with all your heart,And do not rely on your own understanding.

**3:6** In all your ways acknowledge [God],And your paths will be made smooth.

**3:7** Do not be wise in your own eyes;Fear GOD and shun evil.

**3:8** It will be a cure for your body,abody Lit. “navel.” A tonic for your bones.

**3:9** Honor GOD with your wealth,With the best of all your income,

**3:10** And your barns will be filled with grain,Your vats will burst with new wine.

**3:11** Do not reject GOD’s discipline, my son;Do not abhor such rebuke.

**3:12** For whom GOD loves, [God] rebukes,As a father the son whom he favors.

**3:13** Happy is the one who finds wisdom,The one who attains understanding.

**3:14** HerbHer See note at 1.20. value in trade is better than silver,Her yield, greater than gold.

**3:15** She is more precious than rubies;All of your goods cannot equal her.

**3:16** In her right hand is length of days,In her left, riches and honor.

**3:17** Her ways are pleasant ways,And all her paths, peaceful.

**3:18** She is a tree of life to those who grasp her,And whoever holds on to her is happy.

**3:19** 
GOD
 founded the earth by wisdomAnd established the heavens by understanding;

**3:20** By God’s knowledge the depths burst apart,And the skies distilled dew.

**3:21** My son, do not lose sight of them;Hold on to resourcefulness and foresight.

**3:22** They will give life to your spiritAnd grace to your throat.

**3:23** Then you will go your way safelyAnd not injure your feet.

**3:24** When you lie down you will be unafraid;You will lie down and your sleep will be sweet.

**3:25** You will not fear sudden terrorOr the disaster that comes upon the wicked,

**3:26** For GOD will be your trustAnd will keep your feet from being caught.

**3:27** Do not withhold good from those who deserve itWhen you have the power to do it [for them].

**3:28** Do not say to your fellow, “Come back again;I’ll give it to you tomorrow,” when you have it with you.

**3:29** Do not devise harm against your fellowWho lives trustfully with you.

**3:30** Do not quarrel with someone for no cause,When they have done you no harm.

**3:31** Do not envy a lawless man,Or choose any of his ways;

**3:32** For the one who goes astray is an abomination to GOD,But the straightforward are intimates.

**3:33** 
GOD’s curse is on the house of the evildoer,But the abode of the righteous is blessed.

**3:34** At scoffers [God] scoffs,But the lowly are shown grace.

**3:35** The wise shall obtain honor,But dullards get disgrace as their portion.


## Chapter 4

**4:1** Sons, heed the discipline of a father;Listen and learn discernment,

**4:2** For I give you good instruction;Do not forsake my teaching.

**4:3** Once I was a son to my father,The tender darling of my mother.

**4:4** He instructed me and said to me,“Let your mind hold on to my words;Keep my commandments and you will live.

**4:5** Acquire wisdom, acquire discernment;Do not forget and do not swerve from my words.

**4:6** Do not forsake heraher See note at 1.20. and she will guard you;Love her and she will protect you.

**4:7** The beginningbbeginning Or “best part.” of wisdom is—acquire wisdom;With all your acquisitions, acquire discernment.

**4:8** Hug her to you and she will exalt you;She will bring you honor if you embrace her.

**4:9** She will adorn your head with a graceful wreath;Crown you with a glorious diadem.”

**4:10** My son, heed and take in my words,And you will have many years of life.

**4:11** I instruct you in the way of wisdom;I guide you in straight courses.

**4:12** You will walk without breaking stride;When you run, you will not stumble.

**4:13** Hold fast to discipline; do not let go;Keep it; it is your life.

**4:14** Do not enter on the path of the wicked;Do not walk on the way of evildoers.

**4:15** Avoid it; do not pass through it;Turn away from it; pass it by.

**4:16** For they cannot sleep unless they have done evil;Unless they make someone fall they are robbed of sleep.

**4:17** They eat the bread of wickednessAnd drink the wine of lawlessness.

**4:18** The path of the righteous is like radiant sunlight,Ever brightening until noon.

**4:19** The way of the wicked is all darkness;They do not know what will make them stumble.

**4:20** My son, listen to my speech;Incline your ear to my words.

**4:21** Do not lose sight of them;Keep them in your mind.

**4:22** They are life to those who find them,Healing for the whole body.

**4:23** More than all that you guard, guard your mind,For it is the source of life.

**4:24** Put crooked speech away from you;Keep devious talk far from you.

**4:25** Let your eyes look forward,Your gaze be straight ahead.

**4:26** Survey the course you take,And all your ways will prosper.

**4:27** Do not swerve to the right or the left;Keep your feet from evil.


## Chapter 5

**5:1** My son, listen to my wisdom;Incline your ear to my insight,

**5:2** That you may have foresight,While your lips hold fast to knowledge.

**5:3** For the lips of a forbiddenaforbidden Lit. “strange.” woman drip honey;Her mouth is smoother than oil;

**5:4** But in the end she is as bitter as wormwood,Sharp as a two-edged sword.

**5:5** Her feet go down to Death;Her steps take hold of Sheol.

**5:6** She does not chart a path of life;Her course meanders for lack of knowledge.

**5:7** So now, sons, pay heed to me,And do not swerve from the words of my mouth.

**5:8** Keep yourself far away from her;Do not come near the doorway of her house

**5:9** Lest you give up your vigor to others,Your years to a ruthless one;

**5:10** Lest strangers eat their fill of your strength,And your toil be for the house of another;

**5:11** And in the end you roar,When your flesh and body are consumed,

**5:12** And say,“O how I hated discipline,And heartily spurned rebuke.

**5:13** I did not pay heed to my teachers,Or incline my ear to my instructors.

**5:14** Soon I was in dire troubleAmidst the assembled congregation.”

**5:15** Drink water from your own cistern,Running water from your own well.

**5:16** Your springs will gush forthIn streams in the public squares.

**5:17** They will be yours alone,Others having no part with you.

**5:18** Let your fountain be blessed:Find joy in the wife of your youth—

**5:19** A loving doe, a graceful mountain goat.Let her breasts satisfy you at all times;Be infatuated with love of her always.

**5:20** Why be infatuated, my son, with a forbiddenbforbidden Or “a stranger.” woman?Why clasp the bosom of an alien woman?

**5:21** For everyone’s ways are before the eyes of God,Who surveys their entire course.

**5:22** The wicked man will be trapped in his iniquities;He will be caught up in the ropes of his sin.

**5:23** He will die for lack of discipline,Infatuated by his great folly.
