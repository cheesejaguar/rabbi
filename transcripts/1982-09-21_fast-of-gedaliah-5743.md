# Fast of Gedaliah, 3rd Day of Tishrei, 5743

**Date:** September 21, 1982 (3rd Tishrei 5743)
**Occasion:** Fast of Gedaliah
**Source:** Sichos In English, Volume 15

---

1. Public fasts are auspicious times for making resolutions regarding the three pillars on which the world exists â€” Torah, prayer and deeds of loving kindness. The Fast of Gedaliah falls within the Ten Days of Repentance, making community prayer particularly powerful during this observance.

2. Drawing from Psalms chapters 18-20, the discourse emphasizes that Jews achieve victory through divine strength, ultimately leading to redemption through Moshiach. The passages stress G-d's protection and responsiveness to Jewish prayer.

3. The weekly portion Ha'azinu teaches that non-Jewish nations exist "for the sake of the number of the children of Israel." Since Jews are G-d's inheritance and are guarded "as the pupil of His eye," nations should recognize this and assist Jews rather than harm them. One should increase in Torah, prayer, and particularly charitable giving, as these practices "bring near the redemption" and transform fasts into occasions of joy.
