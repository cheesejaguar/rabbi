# 1st Day of Rosh Chodesh Adar, 5740

**Date:** February 17, 1980 (1st Day of Rosh Chodesh Adar, 5740)
**Occasion:** Rosh Chodesh Adar
**Source:** Sichos In English, Volume 5

---

The discourse addresses the principle that "a man should not take leave of his friend except through a statement of Halachah" (Talmud, Berachos 31a). This establishes spiritual connection during physical separation.

The teaching explains that individuals evolve daily in their service of G-d, and similarly, each month brings distinct spiritual tasks. When transitioning between months, a halachic statement maintains continuity of spiritual work.

## The Halachah: Shekalim and Kilayim

The appropriate halachah for this occasion concerns announcements made on the first of Adar regarding:

- **Shekalim**: The half-shekel offering given by all Jewish males
- **Kilayim**: Laws prohibiting mixing certain plant varieties

These represent two complementary Torah principles: unity and individuality. Shekalim emphasizes that all Jews, regardless of wealth, contribute equally -- highlighting collective identity. Kilayim stresses maintaining distinctions and boundaries, recognizing individual uniqueness and separate roles.

The teaching illustrates how these concepts worked in Temple service: Kohanim, Levites, and Israelites each had distinct, non-interchangeable responsibilities, yet functioned as one unified whole.

## Practical Applications

The discourse emphasizes the Previous Rebbe's focus on concrete action, calling for:

- Opening thirty new educational institutions
- Establishing free-loan funds
- Publishing Torah essays
- Spreading the "Ten Campaigns" (Mivtzayim)
- Distributing charitable donations
- Promoting Purim observances

The teaching connects these actions to the thirty-year anniversary of the Previous Rebbe's passing, encouraging increased engagement with Torah study and mitzvah practice.
