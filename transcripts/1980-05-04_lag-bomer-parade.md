# Lag B'Omer -- Address to the Parade

**Date:** May 4, 1980 (18th Day of Iyar, 5740)
**Occasion:** Lag B'Omer, Address to the Parade
**Source:** Sichos In English, Volume 5

---

## Section 1: Jewish Oneness

The gathering demonstrates how children "become one and united with thousands and tens of thousands" despite coming from different lands and cities. Jewish children from different backgrounds, languages, and countries share fundamental unity through Torah and the Ten Commandments. Despite differences in "clothes and foods," all follow the same ethical and spiritual principles.

## Section 2: R. Shimon bar Yochai's Lesson

Drawing on teachings of Rabbi Shimon bar Yochai, the discourse explains that G-d doesn't remain distant but actively dwells "within you" among the Jewish people. The Shechinah (Divine Presence) goes with Jews "in every place where the Jews were exiled," providing strength to observe Torah and mitzvos without being deterred by external challenges.

## Section 3: Counting the Omer

The mitzvah of counting the Omer teaches appreciating each day as precious. Individuals should make every moment "filled with Judaism" through Torah study and commandment observance.

## Section 4: Message to Soviet Jews

The address specifically encourages Russian Jews to maintain faith that they can practice Judaism freely. It references the Russian Constitution's guarantees of religious freedom and encourages teaching children about Torah and mitzvos.

## Section 5: Jerusalem and Israel

The discourse connects joy to remembering Jerusalem, emphasizing that Jews must rely on G-d rather than foreign powers for security and independence in their homeland.
