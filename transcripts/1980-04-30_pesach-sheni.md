# Pesach Sheni

**Date:** April 30, 1980 (14th Day of Iyar, 5740)
**Occasion:** Pesach Sheni
**Source:** Sichos In English, Volume 5

---

## The Concept of Correction

The Rebbe emphasizes that "nothing is ever lost, one can always correct oneself." Even those who were spiritually distant or impure can achieve restoration through repentance (Teshuvah).

## Two Perspectives on Pesach Sheni

The discourse addresses halachic debate regarding whether Pesach Sheni functions as mere compensation for the first Pesach or holds independent festival status. This distinction affects obligations for converts and children who reached majority between the two dates.

## Spiritual Completion (Shleimus)

The Rebbe distinguishes between two meanings of "Mashlim" -- compensating for a deficiency versus achieving fulfillment. Both concepts reveal how spiritual service reaches higher levels through dedicated effort.

## Pesach Sheni and Shemitah

In a Shemitah (sabbatical) year, additional significance emerges. The Rebbe connects this to Lag B'Omer and Rabbi Shimon bar Yochai, whose work focused on correcting and completing the Jewish people's spiritual state.

## Practical Application

The teachings stress that individuals should seize every opportunity to help fellow Jews spiritually and materially, recognizing that one's soul purpose may involve specific acts of kindness impossible to predetermine.
