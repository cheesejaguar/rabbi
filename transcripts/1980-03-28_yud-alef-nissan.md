# Yud-Alef (11th Day of) Nissan, 5740

**Date:** March 28, 1980 (11th Day of Nissan, 5740)
**Occasion:** Yud-Alef Nissan -- The Rebbe's Birthday
**Source:** Sichos In English, Volume 5

---

## Section 1: Torah as the Torah of Life

Torah is called "the Torah of life" containing directives governing every aspect of existence. The text discusses how Torah provides three types of directives: positive commands, prohibitions, and discretionary time. The sicha addresses the principle of opening with blessing and thanking those who conveyed blessings, referencing the Farbrengen as thanksgiving. It mentions blessings for children, health, prosperity, and Pesach preparations, concluding with messianic references.

## Section 2: Children and Pesach Observance

Discusses the connection between children and Pesach observance, emphasizing parental sacrifice for children's spiritual education. The section stresses Jewish education's importance and children's roles during Pesach celebrations. It discusses family unity and the prophecy of "a great congregation will return there."

## Section 3: Address to Children

Addresses children directly, blessing them and outlining their mission as the Jewish people's future. It emphasizes teaching children about Judaism from infancy and their responsibility to bring "life and light" to their homes despite obstacles.

## Section 4: Significance of the Date

Examines the significance of Yud-Alef Nissan falling on Friday during Parshas Tzav. The word Tzav implies "encouragement" applicable to present and future generations. Friday's uniqueness in creation narrative receives extensive analysis, with discussion of "very good" versus "good," emphasizing unlimited potential.

## Section 5: Asher's Tribal Sacrifice

Discusses Asher's tribal sacrifice, connected with pleasure as unlimited potential. The section emphasizes deriving pleasure from Torah study and Mitzvah fulfillment to expand service and hasten redemption.
