# Yisro 5752: Dwelling-Place and the One Who Dwells There

**Date:** Parshas Yisro, 5752 (1992)
**Source:** Dvar Malchus 770 (dvarmalchus770.org)

---

The sicha examines the Ten Commandments and their connection to "Hashem's desire for a dwelling place down below."

Two versions of the Dibros exist: those spoken by Hashem in Parashas Yisro and those repeated by Moshe Rabbeinu in Parashas V'eschanon, representing different spiritual dimensions.

"The true dwelling place for Hashem's essence is the Yidden themselves" because they maintain unity with the Divine essence as stated in Kabbalistic sources.

The first Dibros, heard directly from Hashem, provide power to create "a dwelling place for His Essence." However, their intensity caused listeners' souls to depart. The second version, spoken by Moshe, allowed the Jewish people to receive and internalize the commandments without nullification.

The teaching emphasizes that perfection comes through unity of both dimensions: direct revelation of Divine essence combined with the lower world's capacity to receive and internalize it without being overwhelmed.

The discussion connects to the significance of the 10th and 11th of Shevat, representing numerical mysticism where 10 corresponds to the ten sefirot and 11 represents Keter transcending the sefirotic structure.
