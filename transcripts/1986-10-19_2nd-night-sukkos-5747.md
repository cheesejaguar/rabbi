# 2nd Night Of Sukkos, 5747

**Date:** October 19, 1986 (16 Tishrei, 5747)
**Occasion:** Second Night of Sukkos, Simchas Bais Hashoevah
**Source:** Sichos In English, Volume 33

---

The discourse explores the spiritual significance of the second night of Sukkos. Each day has unique qualities while being part of a larger whole. As noted, "each day has a particular service associated with it that serves as the means to elevate all other services."

The Ushpizin for this night are Patriarch Isaac and the Maggid of Mezeritch, both exemplifying the principle of remaining in one's place while elevating others.

The teaching contrasts two approaches to Divine service -- descending to help others (like Abraham and the Baal Shem Tov) versus elevating from one's position without leaving (like Isaac and the Maggid). Both paths are necessary and complement each other.

The daily psalm portion and Torah readings provide lessons aligned with the day's spiritual focus. Even celebratory dancing in the streets aligns with spiritual service, as "the street... is an integral part of his existence."

The text emphasizes that all Torah concepts can be understood by every Jew, regardless of spiritual level, and that these exile-era celebrations prefigure future Messianic celebrations in the Temple.
