# Antidote to the Exile

**Occasion:** 15th of Av
**Source:** Days of Destiny, Sichos In English

---

The fifteenth of Av stands as one of Judaism's greatest festivals, comparable only to Yom Kippur. The Talmud identifies five primary reasons for its celebration:

## Tribal Unity and Redemption

The day commemorates when inheritance restrictions were lifted, enabling women to marry across tribal lines. Additionally, the tribe of Benjamin was reintegrated into the broader Jewish community after a period of separation, symbolizing the restoration of national cohesion.

## End of Generational Decree

The wilderness generation, condemned to perish during the forty-year desert journey, experienced their final deaths on the ninth of Av. When no deaths occurred on the fifteenth, they recognized the divine decree had concluded.

## Restoration of Pilgrimage

King Hoshea removed roadside guards that had prevented northern kingdom residents from journeying to Jerusalem's Temple during festivals, reunifying divided populations.

## Honoring the Dead

Following Betar's Roman conquest, the fifteenth marked when victims were finally permitted burial -- a significant act of dignity.

## Completion of Sacred Work

Wood-cutting for the Temple altar concluded this day, as summer's heat diminished afterward, affecting wood quality.

## Spiritual Symbolism

"The fifteenth of the month represents when the moon reaches fullness," reflecting Jewish resilience. Just as lunar cycles show waning and waxing, Jewish history demonstrates temporary descent followed by spiritual ascent.

The festival directly counterbalances Tishah B'Av's tragedies, transforming suffering into redemption through the practice of loving-kindness among Jews.
