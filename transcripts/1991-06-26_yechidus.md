# Yechidus

**Date:** June 26, 1991 (14th of Tammuz, 5751)
**Occasion:** Yechidus -- Private Audience
**Source:** Sichos In English, Volume 49

---

The opening addresses peace, beginning with the teaching "Begin with peace." It reflects on the Previous Rebbe's redemption and connects this to themes from Torah portions about the Jewish people's spiritual importance and numerical abundance.

The text uses metaphorical language, comparing Jews to both dust and stars to convey humility alongside significance. It emphasizes concepts like bittul (selflessness) and draws connections to redemption themes, stating that "It is only our bodies which are in exile...our souls were never sent into exile."

The discourse concludes by encouraging increased Torah study and charitable giving, linking these practices to bringing about ultimate redemption.

## Address to Bar and Bas Mitzvah

Special blessings are offered to young people reaching religious maturity. The section emphasizes the custom of giving extra charity on this occasion and making strong commitments to observing commandments.

## Address to Grooms and Brides

This section blesses couples preparing for marriage, describing weddings as creating "an eternal structure with its foundation on the Torah and its mitzvos." It connects personal celebrations to messianic themes and encourages charitable donations in connection with weddings.

The discourse concludes by suggesting this period can transform from solemn observance into joy and celebration.
