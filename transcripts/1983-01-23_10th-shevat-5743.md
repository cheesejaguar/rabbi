# 10th Day of Shevat, 5743

**Date:** January 23, 1983 (10th Shevat 5743)
**Occasion:** 10th of Shevat / Yartzeit of Previous Rebbe
**Source:** Sichos In English, Volume 16

---

This discourse addresses collective Jewish purpose and individual responsibility. The Rebbe explains that "each person must see himself as part of the entire creation, charged by G-d with the task of working with that creation and elevating it to its ultimate state."

The address emphasizes transforming the world into "a dwelling place for G-d in the lower worlds." This vision encompasses both personal obligation and communal cooperation, reflecting the principle that "man is a social being" who achieves greater accomplishment through unified effort.

Regarding education and morality, the Rebbe advocates teaching children fundamental awareness of divine sovereignty. He specifically addresses the American constitutional principle of church-state separation, asserting that recognizing "G-d exists" and that "the world has a Master" forms the foundation for ethical behavior.

The discourse notes that "only through an awareness of G-d" can children develop moral restraint and proper conduct. The Rebbe proposes two immediate educational measures: government support for parochial schools and instituting moments of silence for meditation on divine existence.

The address concludes by connecting the previous Rebbe's teaching that "America is no different" -- meaning essential spiritual mission transcends geographical circumstances -- to contemporary Jewish life and practice.
