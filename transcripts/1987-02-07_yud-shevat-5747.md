# Yud Shevat, 5747

**Date:** February 7, 1987 (10 Shevat, 5747)
**Occasion:** Yartzeit of the Previous Rebbe; Anniversary of Leadership
**Source:** Sichos In English, Volume 34

---

## 1 - Blessing Through Gathering

The presence of multiple Jews creates spiritual blessing. The gathering occurs at a synagogue and study hall. Though marked by the yartzeit (anniversary of passing) of the Previous Rebbe, this occasion provides opportunity for reflection and repentance. Even children absorb the special atmosphere, and increased charitable giving on such occasions has practical, far-reaching effects.

## 2 - Living with the Times

Drawing from the weekly Torah portion Beshallach, the speaker explains how Jews should influence non-Jews to observe the Seven Noachide Commandments. Despite being numerically smaller, Jewish efforts succeed because they fulfill divine mission. The portion teaches that obstacles need not prevent this work, as "G-d only makes demands in accordance with our capabilities."

The discourse also addresses paradoxes regarding Moshiach's arrival and peace-building, explaining that positive mitzvos should be extended while negative ones require only minimum fulfillment.

## 3 - Establishing Houses of Study

Permanent institutional structures for Torah, prayer, and charity are emphasized over temporary ones. The teaching stresses that actual construction matters more than discussion alone.

## 4 - Mishneh Torah Analysis

This section examines the Rambam's discussion of Eliyahu the prophet's arrival before Moshiach, resolving apparent disagreements by distinguishing between timing and status.

## 5 - CHITAS Practice

The discourse concludes by promoting daily study of Torah portion, Psalms, and Tanya combined (CHITAS), encouraging those who fell behind to resume and increase their learning.
