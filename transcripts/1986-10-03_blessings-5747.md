# Blessings -- 5747

**Date:** October 3-17, 1986 (Erev Rosh Hashanah, Erev Yom Kippur, Erev Sukkos, 5747)
**Occasion:** Blessings before major holidays
**Source:** Sichos In English, Volume 33

---

## Blessings after Pidyon Nefesh -- Erev Rosh Hashanah

The Rebbe discusses relying on the presumption that all requests written in the Pidyon are favorable, emphasizing that G-d will fulfill the pleas of the Jewish people, particularly regarding the coming of the righteous Moshiach and redemption.

## Blessings -- Erev Yom Kippur

This section explores the spiritual significance of the day preceding Yom Kippur. Key themes include:

- Yom Kippur's connection to the Yechidah (highest level of the Jewish soul)
- The concept of "teshuvah ila'ah" (higher repentance) as union with G-d
- The relationship between Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur in revealing divine unity
- The special character of 5747 as both beginning on Shabbat and being a Shemittah (Sabbatical) year
- Connection between physical and spiritual blessing

The Rebbe includes an extended blessing using the Hebrew alphabet format, requesting blessings of light, gladness, splendor, unity, abundant produce, miracles, redemption, and praise.

## Blessings -- Erev Yom Kippur to the Students

Addressed to Yeshivas Tomchei Temimim students, emphasizing Torah study as connected to the Kohen Gadol service and Yechidah consciousness, particularly the mission to spread Chassidic teachings.

## Blessing -- Erev Sukkos

A brief blessing requesting success and the drawing of "good radiance" through the four kinds of Sukkot, emphasizing Jewish unity and the coming redemption.
