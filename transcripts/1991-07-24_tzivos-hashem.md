# Tzivos Hashem

**Date:** July 24, 1991 (13th Day of Menachem Av, 5751)
**Occasion:** Address to Children -- Tzivos Hashem Rally
**Source:** Sichos In English, Volume 49

---

The speaker emphasizes that "children who have been raised and educated in a Jewish manner should serve as an example" to those lacking proper Jewish education, particularly in remote areas.

The discourse connects Jewish children to the giving of Torah, noting that children serve as spiritual guarantors for their people. This concept "effects one's body and soul, causing one to study Torah and fulfill mitzvos."

The Ten Commandments are highlighted as containing 620 letters, corresponding to the 613 Torah commandments and seven rabbinic commandments. Special emphasis is placed on the obligation to respect others' property: "not only silver and gold and objects of value, but even objects of minimal worth which another Jew owns, must be cared for."

Throughout, the discourse connects these actions to hastening redemption and the rebuilding of the Holy Temple in Jerusalem, with repeated references to gathering "adults and children together" in celebration.
