# Vayigash 5752: The World's Limitations Are No Obstacle

**Date:** Parshas Vayigash, 5752 (1992)
**Source:** Dvar Malchus 770 (dvarmalchus770.org)

---

In the Torah portion, Yehuda approaches Yosef (Egypt's viceroy) without seeking permission to demand Binyomin's release. Although Yosef held greater worldly power, Yehuda demonstrated superior spiritual strength through his willingness to risk everything for his brother.

The Rebbe distinguishes two approaches to worldly existence: being constrained by physical and social limitations, versus transcending such boundaries entirely through connection to the Divine.

Using the Purim narrative as analogy, the Rebbe explains how King Achashverosh's feast fulfilled each person's desires -- Mordechai's (rejecting worldly idolatry) and Haman's (accepting golus limitations) -- simultaneously. This appears impossible in ordinary governance but becomes achievable through connection with the Almighty, allowing one to simultaneously exist within and rise above worldly constraints.

## Key Teaching

"In our generation this is entirely dependent on a Jew's will." Previous generations faced external persecution limiting religious observance. Today's generation enjoys unprecedented freedom to practice Judaism openly without governmental restriction.

The current era uniquely permits Jews to maintain spiritual integrity while participating fully in society. Nations actively assist Jewish religious and educational endeavors. Therefore, Jews need not employ deception or compromise -- authentic practice and worldly engagement are compatible.

## Conclusion

The Frierdicker Rebbe confirmed that Redemption preparations are complete; implementation depends solely upon Jewish commitment. Each person should conduct themselves with confidence that "the world was created for me" to fulfill Torah commandments and bring Redemption into revealed reality.
