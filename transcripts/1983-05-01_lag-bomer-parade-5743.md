# Lag B'Omer - At Parade, 18th Day of Iyar, 5743

**Date:** May 1, 1983 (18th Iyar 5743)
**Occasion:** Lag B'Omer Parade
**Source:** Sichos In English, Volume 17

---

There are two principal reasons for celebrating Lag B'Omer:

1. It commemorates Rabbi Shimon bar Yochai's passing.
2. It marks when Rabbi Akiva's disciples "ceased to die" after treating each other with proper respect.

The discourse emphasizes that followers should derive practical guidance from Lag B'Omer observance. One central message states: "Love your fellow as yourself" represents "a great principle in Torah," requiring both positive action and restraint from harmful behavior.

Regarding Torah Study: The text teaches that individuals should view Torah as their primary occupation, even when engaged in other necessary activities. Children especially should maximize time for religious study during summer breaks through organized camps.

On Interpersonal Conduct: The material contrasts two approaches -- actively performing good deeds and conscientiously avoiding disrespectful or harmful treatment. An example illustrates how one should not prioritize personal desires over others' prior claims, even if one has previously helped that person.

The discourse connects these lessons to Jewish unity and anticipates redemption, culminating in a prayer for rebuilding the Temple and distributing charitable coins.
