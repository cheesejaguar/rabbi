# Shabbos Parshas Vayakhel-Pikudei, Parshas Hachodesh

**Date:** March 14, 1980 (27th Day of Adar, 5740)
**Occasion:** Shabbos Parshas Vayakhel-Pikudei, Parshas Hachodesh, Mevorchim Chodesh Nissan
**Source:** Sichos In English, Volume 5

---

## The New Month Concept

The discourse explains that the "birth of the new moon" represents "revelation of new light after concealment." Each month requires spiritual advancement where previous accomplishments appear eclipsed by newly achieved heights.

## Unity and Individuality

The two Torah portions present seemingly opposite ideas. Vayakhel emphasizes "fusion of individual beings into one community," while Pikudei stresses counting individuals separately. Both are reconciled through Moshe's level of self-nullification, enabling simultaneous collective unity and individual recognition.

## Radical Progress

The teaching emphasizes that daily service should involve "radical advances" rather than mere gradual progression. This aligns with Pesach's meaning -- "to leap" -- representing transformative spiritual jumps.
